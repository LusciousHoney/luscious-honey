# The Executive Operating System — Charter

**Status:** Approved architecture. The organizing instrument of the EOS constitutional volume.
**Role:** governs *organization and boundary*, not content. It ranks, indexes, and reconciles
the constitutional documents; it does not restate or replace them.
**Supersedes:** nothing. **Duplicates:** nothing.

This Charter is the front matter of the Executive Operating System. It fixes the permanent
hierarchy, the canonical terminology, and which document governs what — so the individual
constitutions can be read, and later implemented, without contradiction.

---

## I. What the EOS is

The Executive Operating System is the whole: **two permanent operating engines and one
permanent bridge.**

- **Headquarters** — manages the institution.
- **The Executive Manager** — manages the Founder.
- **The Chief of Staff** — the only permanent bridge between them.

The two engines are **co-equal domains under the Founder**. Neither outranks the other; each
is sovereign in its domain. The Charter organizes both; it governs neither engine's internals.

---

## II. The permanent hierarchy

```
                    Founder                         ← ultimate authority
                       │
             Executive Manager                      ← manages the Founder (Founder engine)
                       │
             Chief of Staff  (Chair #001)           ← the only permanent bridge
                       │
                 Headquarters                        ← manages the institution (institution engine)
                       │
        The Chairs / executive functions            ← Creative Director #002 · Head of Production #003
                                                       Director of Growth #004 · (Business function)
```

- **The Founder** is the ultimate authority. Every chain of decision and delegation
  terminates upward at the Founder and nowhere else.
- **The Executive Manager** is the Founder's engine — attention, time, coordination, personal
  executive support. Its permanent client is the Founder.
- **The Chief of Staff** is Chair #001 *and* the sole bridge — the one role standing in both
  worlds. There is no second bridge.
- **Headquarters** is the institution's engine, run by the **Council of Chairs**.
- **The Chairs / executive functions** are the institution's executive roles (Creative,
  Production, Growth, and the Business function). In the residence they are experienced as
  **wings / studios / suites** (see the Experience Architecture). This hierarchy does not
  change, and no new organizational layer is added to reconcile vocabulary.

## V. The boundary the Charter guards

One line is load-bearing and permanent:

> **Headquarters manages the institution. The Executive Manager manages the Founder. The
> Chief of Staff bridges the two. Personal life is the Executive Manager's to coordinate —
> privately, and never inside any institution.**

- **No engine reads the other's mind.** Institutional record (Register, Archive,
  Recommendations) is not Founder memory; Founder memory never enters an institution.
- **One owner per interaction.** Ownership is never shared across the bridge (Bridge
  Contract, §"the prime rule").
- **One source of truth per fact.** Recommendations remain the single source of institutional
  decisions; the Executive Work Queue remains a *derived* projection; no parallel store is
  created to reconcile vocabulary or convenience.
- **Additive change only.** New documents, worlds, or capabilities extend this structure;
  they do not blur the boundary. A proposal that would cross it is surfaced to the Founder as
  a conflict *before* it is built.

## VI. Ratification

This Charter takes effect when the Founder ratifies the EOS volume as canon. It creates no
functionality, interface, or code. It is the permanent index and boundary-keeper for
everything the Executive Operating System will become.
