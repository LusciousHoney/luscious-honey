# Editorial Copy Review — Journal & Writing Wall orientation

Copy-only pass. No CSS, routes, layout, or features changed. Goal: a first-time
visitor understands *what these are* without over-explaining; mystery and elegance
preserved.

## Changes made (copy only)

| Place | Before | After | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| `press.html` — Journal dek | "The founder's notes from the desk. Only published entries appear here; nothing writes itself." | "Notes from the desk, in the founder's own hand — a page is added only when something inside the House is worth remembering, never to a calendar. Nothing here writes itself." | Obj 1 + 5: implies *not a blog* (own hand) and *not scheduled* (never to a calendar; only when worth remembering). No banned words. |
| `publishing.html` — Writing Wall intro (new line, existing `.editorial` style) | *(no intro)* | "Fragments — lines caught mid-thought, kept before they become finished work. New ones appear as they emerge, not by any calendar." | Obj 2 + 5: names them as unfinished fragments; cadence without a schedule. Two sentences, poetic. |
| `publishing.html` — wing intro | "…the editor's desk, the writing wall, and the pieces…" | "…the editor's desk and its House Journal, the Writing Wall, and the pieces…" | Obj 3 + 4: surfaces the proper feature names so they read as institutional features. |

The Writing Wall line uses the existing `.editorial` type — no new style, no layout
change.

## Obj 3 — "What is this?" audit (recommendations)

| Space | First-timer risk | Recommendation |

No layout, no new features — recommendations only, as scoped.

## Obj 4 — Naming review

| Name | Verdict | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **House Journal** | **Keep.** | Institutional, warm, and accurate — a journal is dated and personal, not a feed. Strong. |
| **Writing Wall** | **Keep.** | Evokes a physical plaster wall of fragments; distinctly non-blog. Strong. |
| **Journal Archive** (door blurb / press link) | **Keep.** | "Archive" signals permanence and depth, on-brand. Strong. |

No renames recommended — clarity is not *significantly* improved by any change, and
the institutional tone is best preserved by keeping these established names.

## Obj 5 — Cadence language

Both features now communicate an event-driven, non-calendar rhythm **without** the
forbidden vocabulary (`weekly`, `monthly`, `blog`, `news`, `feed`):

- Journal — "added only when something inside the House is worth remembering, never
  to a calendar."
- Writing Wall — "New ones appear as they emerge, not by any calendar."

Phrasings imply the idea editorially rather than stating rules FAQ-style.
