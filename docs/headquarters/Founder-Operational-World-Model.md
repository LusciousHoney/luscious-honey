# The Founder Operational World Model

**Status:** Approved architecture. Governance canon, not yet implemented.
**Part of:** The Executive Operating System (EOS) — Document 3 of 4.
**Defines:** every operational world the Executive Manager coordinates on the Founder's
behalf — and the permanent line between *coordinating a world* and *operating it*.
**Depends on:** [[Executive-Manager-Constitution]].

This document defines behavior; it does not implement it. No code, store, or interface is
created here.

---

## The doctrine

> **The Executive Manager manages the Founder's relationship to each world. It never
> operates the world. Institutions are operated by their own Headquarters; the Executive
> Manager coordinates the one thing all the worlds share — the Founder.**

The Founder stands at the center of many worlds. Each makes claims on one finite person's
time, attention, and energy. The Executive Manager is the only layer that sees them all at
once — and it holds them together at the level of the *Founder*, never by reaching inside
any world's operations.

---

## The two verbs — and why the difference is permanent

Every world is touched with exactly one of two verbs, and the Executive Manager only ever
uses the first:

- **Coordinate** *(Executive Manager)* — manage the Founder's *relationship* to a world:
  how much attention it gets, when it reaches the Founder, how it trades off against the
  others, what it costs in energy and time.
- **Operate** *(the world's own Headquarters, or the Founder directly)* — do the world's
  actual work: produce, decide, record, execute.

The Executive Manager coordinating a world must **never** drift into operating it. That
drift is the failure this model exists to prevent: the moment the Executive Manager starts
making an institution's decisions, the boundary in the
[[Executive-Manager-Constitution]] has been breached.

---

## World classification

Each world is one of two kinds. The kind determines how it reaches the Founder.

- **Instituted worlds** have (or will have) their own Headquarters and their own bridge
  (a Chief of Staff or equivalent). The Executive Manager sees them *only* through that
  bridge and never operates them.
- **Direct worlds** have no institution of their own. They reach the Founder directly
  through the Executive Manager, which coordinates them but still does not "operate" them in
  any institutional sense — it simply holds the Founder's relationship to them.

## The worlds

### Luscious Honey Collective — *instituted*
The flagship institution. Fully operated by its own Headquarters (the six departments, the
Executive Council, the Chief of Staff). The Executive Manager coordinates only the
Founder's relationship to it — how much Founder attention it draws, when its decisions
reach the Founder — and sees it exclusively through the Chief of Staff. **The Executive
Manager never touches LHC operations.**

### Pull Me Under — *instituted (within the LHC universe)*
A production/property within the Luscious Honey universe; a destination in its future
*Productions* wing. Operationally it runs as institutional work and reaches the Founder as
such, through the same bridge. The Executive Manager coordinates the Founder's attention to
it; it does not run the residence.

### HR Baddie Society — *instituted (standalone)*
A separate, standalone venture with its own footing. Its internal operations are its own.
The Executive Manager coordinates the Founder's *relationship* to it — the demands it
places on Founder time and attention alongside everything else — without absorbing its
operations into any Headquarters.

### WEA / union responsibilities — *direct*
The day-job track: the manual, recurring duties of the Founder's union employment. Real
obligations with real-world consequences, protected on their own schedule and honored as
first-class. The Executive Manager coordinates them into the Founder's week and guards
their deadlines; it does not perform the duties and never treats them as institutional
work of the Collective.

### Personal life — *direct*
Commitments, relationships, health, rest. Not a residual to be filled in around work —
first-class, and the most privacy-protected world (see
[[Executive-Memory-and-Context-Constitution]]). The Executive Manager coordinates the
Founder's personal commitments and protects the time and energy they require. It is never
surfaced to any institution.

### Travel — *direct, cross-cutting*
Travel spans every other world and costs both time *and* energy. The Executive Manager
coordinates it as logistics and as an attention/energy event — preparing for it, protecting
around it, and reconciling it against every world's claims. Travel is where cross-world
coordination is most visible.

### Future ventures — *provisional*
Worlds not yet formed. The moment a prospective venture begins to draw Founder attention —
before it has any structure of its own — the Executive Manager coordinates that draw. When
and if such a venture becomes an institution, it gains its own Headquarters and bridge and
moves to *instituted*; until then the Executive Manager simply holds the Founder's
relationship to the idea.

## Cross-world coordination

The worlds do not coordinate with each other. They each draw on **one Founder** — that
shared resource is the only thing they have in common, and coordinating that draw is the
Executive Manager's defining work.

1. **The Founder is the sole shared resource.** The Executive Manager coordinates the
   Founder's finite attention, time, and energy *across* the worlds — reconciling, for
   instance, an LHC decision, a WEA duty, and a personal commitment that all land on the
   same day. This is an *attention* problem, and attention belongs to the Executive Manager.
2. **Currencies are the Executive Manager's own, never a brand's workflow.** Coordination is
   expressed in priority, calendar, and energy — never by entering a world's operational
   system. The Executive Manager rebalances the Founder's week; it does not run anyone's
   production pipeline.
3. **Each instituted world keeps its own memory and operations.** Personal management is
   never absorbed into any Headquarters, and no institution's records leak into another.
   LHC's Headquarters does not schedule the WEA day; HRBS does not see the Founder's travel.
4. **One coherent picture, owned by no world.** The output is a single, calm view of a whole
   life — assembled from many worlds, owned by none of them, held only for the Founder.

---

## Boundaries (what stays true for every world)

- **Coordinate, never operate.** The Executive Manager never makes an institution's
  decisions, produces its work, or holds its records.
- **Instituted worlds are seen only through their bridge.** No direct Executive-Manager-to-
  department line, ever.
- **Direct worlds never become institutions by accident.** WEA, personal life, and travel
  are coordinated, not institutionalized; they get no Headquarters and no workflow engine.
- **No world reaches the Founder around the Executive Manager.** Instituted worlds arrive
  via their Chief of Staff; direct worlds arrive via the Executive Manager. There is no
  fourth path.
- **Adding a world is additive.** A new world is classified (instituted / direct /
  provisional) and coordinated under these same rules; it never rewrites the model or
  merges two worlds' operations.

## What this document does not do

It enumerates and classifies the worlds and fixes the coordinate-vs-operate line. It builds
no world model in code, no data, no interface. Implementation is Phase IV (the World Layer)
of [[Executive-Operating-System-Roadmap]], after the Constitutional, Cognitive, and Bridge
layers exist.
