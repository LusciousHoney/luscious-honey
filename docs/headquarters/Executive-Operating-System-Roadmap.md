# The Executive Operating System — Roadmap

**Status:** Approved architecture. Governance canon, not yet implemented.
**Part of:** The Executive Operating System (EOS) — Document 4 of 4.
**Defines:** the permanent order in which the EOS is built. It authorizes no
implementation; it fixes the sequence future sprints must follow.
**Depends on:** Documents 1–3 and [[Executive-Manager-Constitution]].

This document defines behavior; it does not implement it. No phase begins by virtue of
being written here — each is a separate, Founder-approved sprint.

---

## What the EOS is

> **The Executive Operating System is the whole. It has two permanent operating engines
> and one permanent bridge.**

- **Headquarters** — manages the institution.
- **Executive Manager** — manages the Founder.
- **The Chief of Staff** — the only permanent bridge between them.

```
   Founder
      │
   Executive Manager      ┐
      │                   │  EOS
   Chief of Staff         │  (two engines, one bridge)
      │                   │
   Headquarters           ┘
      │
   The Chairs / executive functions
```

This hierarchy is foundational and does not change. The roadmap below builds *outward* from
it; it never rearranges it.

---

## Sequencing principles

The order is not arbitrary. Four invariants govern it:

1. **Constitution before cognition before connection before coordination.** Rules first,
   then the mind that follows them, then the bridge that carries them, then the worlds they
   span.
2. **Models before surfaces.** Every capability is proven as pure, testable logic before any
   interface is built. UI is always last within a phase — never first. (This mirrors how
   Headquarters itself was built.)
3. **The boundary before any wiring.** No engine is connected to another until the boundary
   between them is ratified canon.
4. **Each phase ships as its own reviewed sprint** that stops before merge and deploy, per
   the standing flow. A later phase never begins before its predecessor is complete and
   approved.

## Phase I — The Constitutional Layer

*Fix the rules and the boundaries.* **(Largely drafted.)**

- Headquarters (the institutional engine) — already established and operating in design.
- Executive Manager (the Founder engine) — [[Executive-Manager-Constitution]].
- Authority — the four graduated tiers and the safety floor.
- Founder Attention — the six-disposition model.

**Exit criterion:** the Constitution and this EOS volume are *ratified* as canon. Nothing
downstream may begin until the boundary is fixed. This is the load-bearing phase.

---

## Phase II — The Cognitive Layer

*Give the Executive Manager a mind that obeys the Constitution.*

- Memory — per [[Executive-Memory-and-Context-Constitution]]: the classed, owned,
  expiring, correctable memory, kept permanently separate from institutional memory.
- Context — temporary and expiring working context for the present period.
- Judgment — the pure classification logic behind the six dispositions, and the reasoning
  that turns what is known about the Founder into what reaches the Founder.

**Built as pure, testable logic — no UI.** **Exit criterion:** memory, context, and the
attention/judgment models exist and are proven in isolation, honoring every privacy and
boundary rule of Document 1.

---

## Phase III — The Bridge Layer

*Connect the two engines through the one permanent bridge.*

- Executive Manager ⇄ Chief of Staff ⇄ Headquarters, implemented to the
  [[Bridge-Contract-Executive-Manager-Chief-of-Staff]].
- The information, decision, approval, scheduling, delegation, escalation, conflict, and
  failure flows — each with exactly one owner.

**Exit criterion:** institutional matters can be prepared by the Chief of Staff and enter
the Executive Manager's attention model, and Founder decisions can flow back — with no
duplicated ownership and no path that skips the bridge.

---

## Phase IV — The World Layer

*Extend coordination across the Founder's worlds.*

- The operational worlds of [[Founder-Operational-World-Model]] — LHC, Pull Me Under, HR
  Baddie Society, WEA, personal life, travel, future ventures — each classified and
  coordinated.
- Cross-world coordination — reconciling the many worlds' claims on one Founder, in the
  Executive Manager's own currencies of priority, calendar, and energy.

**Exit criterion:** the Executive Manager coordinates the Founder's relationship to every
world without operating any of them, and without personal management entering any
Headquarters.

---

## Phase V — Operationalization

*The Executive Manager begins doing its daily work.* Only now does behavior become
operational. Capabilities, each resting on the layers below it:

- Morning Briefings — the day understood before the Founder arrives.
- Attention filtering — the six dispositions, applied continuously.
- Scheduling — calendar and deep-work protection.
- Executive planning — sequencing the Founder's own commitments.
- Context switching — minimizing and batching the costly jumps between worlds.
- Priority balancing — reconciling claims across worlds.
- Conflict detection — surfacing collisions before they reach the Founder as chaos.
- Waiting-work resurfacing — bringing deferred matters back at the right moment.
- Travel preparation — logistics and energy, prepared ahead.
- Meeting preparation — context assembled before the Founder walks in.
- Energy management — treating energy, not just hours, as the constrained resource.

**Exit criterion:** the Executive Operating System is *functioning* — a living executive
layer, not a static interface.

---

## Phase VI — Experience Standardization

*Standardize the experience only once the system is alive.*

Headquarters Experience Standardization — the pending visual/composition/layout work — is
deliberately **last**. The Founder should evaluate Headquarters while it is operating as a
**living executive residence**, with the Executive Operating System running above it, rather
than as a static interface judged in isolation. Standardizing the look before the system
lives would standardize the wrong thing.

**Exit criterion:** with the EOS functioning, the Founder evaluates and standardizes the
experience of the whole living system.

## The shape of the whole

| Phase | Layer | Builds | Surface? |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | Constitutional | rules, boundaries, authority, attention | none |
| II | Cognitive | memory, context, judgment | none (pure logic) |
| III | Bridge | the contract's flows | none (logic + wiring) |
| IV | World | worlds + cross-world coordination | none (logic) |
| V | Operationalization | the daily executive capabilities | yes — the living layer |
| VI | Experience Standardization | the standardized experience | yes — judged while alive |

Surfaces arrive late and deliberately. Everything before Phase V is rules and reasoning.

---

## What this document does not do

It fixes the order; it starts nothing. No phase is authorized by this roadmap — each begins
only as its own Founder-approved sprint, in this sequence, models before surfaces, boundary
before wiring, stopping before merge and deploy. This sprint establishes the constitutional
architecture that will govern all future Executive Operating System development, and no more.
