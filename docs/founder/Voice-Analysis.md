Then I complete every section below from the real text — direct quotes, cited by
question id — with no invention.

## Framework (to be filled from the real packet)

Each section will cite the founder's own words (question id + excerpt), not
paraphrase-as-fact.

1. **Recurring themes** — ideas that surface across ≥3 answers.
2. **Recurring language** — repeated words/phrasings; her natural diction.
3. **Values** — what she rewards and refuses, stated and implied.
4. **Worldview** — the underlying model of creativity, people, time.
5. **Recurring imagery** — the metaphors she reaches for (house, light, hands…).
6. **Emotional patterns** — where she warms, hardens, hesitates.
7. **Strongest stories** — the 2–4 answers with real scene and stakes.
8. **Strongest quotes** — publish-ready lines, verbatim, with source id.
9. **Contradictions** — places two answers pull against each other.
10. **Unanswered questions** — the 2 skipped, plus implicit gaps.
11. **Weak answers needing follow-up** — thin/abstract responses to revisit.
12. **Opportunities for future interviews** — threads worth their own session.

## Method (so the filled version is honest)

- Quote, don't summarize; every claim carries a `fn-…` citation.
- Separate *observed* (her words) from *editor's inference* (labeled as such).
- Flag anything uncertain rather than smoothing it into a clean narrative.
- Cross-reference [Writing-Candidates.md](Writing-Candidates.md) so strong quotes
  route to the right future document.
