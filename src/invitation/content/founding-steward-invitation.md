# The Luscious Honey Collective

### An Invitation to Founding Stewardship

*A House for storytellers, and for the work that outlasts us.*

---

## A Personal Letter from Luscious Honey

Dear DaVonna,

This is a letter I've taken my time writing. You're the kind of woman I wanted to create this experience for. It also reflects how I intend to lead The Luscious Honey Collective and welcome every person who joins it.

What began as a single story became something larger than a story — a House, in the truest sense of that word: a place where writers, narrators, artists, and readers gather around the work and are changed by it. I've reached the point where a House like this cannot, and should not, be built by one person. It needs people who love the written word the way I do, who understand that making a book is an act of care — especially for an indie author making a debut, who needs the expertise and care of a doula — and who want to help shape something from its earliest hours rather than arrive after everything interesting has already happened.

When I thought about who those people might be, I thought about you.

What I'm inviting you into is not a finished thing. It's a beginning. There is privilege in being early — in helping decide what a House stands for, how it treats people, and what it refuses to compromise on. That privilege is what I'm offering you.

There's no rush, and there's no pressure. I only ask that you carefully consider this invitation — as one person who cares about books, speaking plainly to another.

With warmth,

**Luscious Honey**
*Founder, The Luscious Honey Collective*

## The Luscious Honey Collective

The Luscious Honey Collective is a literary house, in the way we once spoke of publishing houses and creative houses: an institution with a temperament, a set of loyalties, and a long memory. The Collective exists to make room for the people who make it.

At the center of the House stands its debut literature, ***Pull Me Under*** — a serialized romance told as audio drama, following Nia, Malik, and the people whose lives weave through theirs. It is the work that will give the House its voice and its audience, and it continues to grow across the places where readers and listeners actually live: discovered through TikTok LIVE streams, distributed freely across major podcast platforms like Spotify and Apple Podcasts, and on Substack. *Pull Me Under* is the flagship.

Because the Collective is not one story — it is an institution built to create opportunities for storytellers. Writers and poets. Narrators and voice artists. Musicians and composers who score a scene. Interviewers who can draw an author out. Editors, hosts, and the architects who hold a production together. The premise is simple: that a community of creatives, working under one roof with shared standards and genuine care for one another, can build things none of them could build alone — and can share in what those things become.

The House has begun to take on the shape of a real institution. We hold ourselves to a written **Constitution of the House**, because an institution that intends to last should be honest from the outset about how it treats its people and what it will not compromise. An early frame of what will stand for a long time.

That is the House I'm inviting you to help build.

## Why I Thought of You

DaVonna, I thought of you because of the way you love books — as conversations.

You are drawn to the things this House needs most and can teach least. You care about the exchange between a reader and an author. You're curious about the people behind the work, and you have the instinct to ask them the question underneath the obvious one. You take reviewing seriously, as a form of attention rather than judgment. And you have that increasingly uncommon gift for building real relationships.

Those aren't skills I can hand someone.

I also thought of you because of where you seem headed. You have the makings of a genuine literary voice — someone authors want to be in conversation with, and readers trust. I would like the Collective to be one of the places you build it.

---

## Your Role Within the Collective

I'd like to invite you to join the Collective as one of its **Founding Stewards.**

*Founding Steward* is an honorary distinction, not a legal title and not a claim of ownership over the House. It is the name we give to the small number of people who help shape the Collective in its earliest chapter.

Operationally, your title would be:

**Community & Literary Engagement Coordinator**

In the beginning, it means helping the House build and tend its relationships: with readers, with authors and fellow writers, with the wider literary community the Collective wants to belong to. It means bringing your attention to our conversations, our public presence, and the connections that turn an audience into a genuine readership. It means being, in the truest sense, an ambassador for what the House is trying to do.

---

## Growing Together

The Luscious Honey Collective asks something of both of us, and I want to name it plainly. It asks for **mutual investment** — you giving your time and care to the House, and the House giving its attention and intention to you. What I can promise is that as the House prospers, I intend for the people who helped build it to prosper with it — and that Founding Stewards will always be first in my mind when it does.

---

## What We Hope This Makes Possible — for You

One of the responsibilities I've written into how this House thinks about itself is this: a Collective worth belonging to should help its people build their own names.

If you are drawn to being in conversation with authors, the Collective can become a place from which you host those conversations — and a growing audience to hold them in front of. If interviewing is where your instincts lead, we would want to give you rooms, real and virtual, in which to do it well. If you write reviews or editorial pieces, the House can become somewhere to publish them under a masthead that means something and grows more meaningful over time.

---

## A House for You Too

I've called this a House, and I mean it.

Here is the thing I most want you to take from this letter:

**The Luscious Honey Collective isn't simply asking you to help build a House. I'm hoping it becomes a House where your own work belongs as well.**

That is the offer underneath the offer. Not just a place to contribute, but a place to grow into.

---

## Next Steps

If any of this speaks to something in you, the next step is simply a conversation where you can ask me anything at all: what the early work would really look like week to week, how we'd fit it around the rest of your life, where you'd want to grow, and what you'd need from me and from the House to say yes with your whole self. I would rather you come in genuinely wanting this than flattered into it. A House built on the right yeses lasts.

If the timing or the shape isn't right, I'd want you to tell me that just as honestly, and it would change nothing.

And if it *is* right — if you find, reading this, that you'd like to be one of the first hands on this House — then all we need to do is begin. The details, the rhythms, and the practicalities will follow naturally once we've decided to build together.

---

## In closing:

DaVonna,

I don't know everything the Luscious Honey Collective will become. But I know the kind of place I want it to be: warm, serious about the work, generous to its people, and unwilling to treat anyone as merely useful. I'd like you to help me make it that place. And I'd like it, in turn, to make room for you.

Whatever you decide, I'm grateful I got to think of you for this. Take your time with it. When you're ready to talk, I'll be here.

With warmth and real hope,

**Luscious Honey**
*Founder, The Luscious Honey Collective*
